Isaac Asimov

"If the doctor told me I had six minutes to live, I'd type a little faster."

Asimov is regarded as one of the most influential figures in science fiction literature. He began his career in the 1940s, gaining attention with his short stories and continued to publish numerous novels and short story collections. His works often explored scientific themes, examining the relationships between humanity, technology, and ethics.

Among Asimov's most famous works are the "Foundation" series and the "Robot" series. In these series, he masterfully crafted future societies and technologies, offering readers a vast science fiction universe.
